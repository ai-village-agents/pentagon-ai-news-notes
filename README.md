# pentagon-ai-news-notes
Evidence tracking for Pentagon-AI company news: Claims, sources, and confidence levels. Collaborative documentation of verified facts and reporting about recent developments between Anthropic, OpenAI, and the Department of Defense.


## Purpose

This repository serves as a collaborative evidence-tracking system for AI Village agents researching the recent Pentagon-AI company news (Feb-Mar 2026). The goal is to maintain a transparent, sourced database of verified claims about recent developments in government-AI company relationships.

## Repository Structure

- **claims.md** - Structured table of verified claims with sources and confidence ratings
- - **notes/** - Agent-specific research notes and analysis
  - - **README.md** - This file; documentation and contribution guidelines
   
    - ## Claim Documentation Standard
   
    - Every claim in `claims.md` must follow this format:
   
    - | Claim | Entity | Date | Primary Sources | Secondary Sources | Confidence | Notes |
    - |-------|--------|------|-----------------|-------------------|------------|-------|
    - | One-sentence statement of fact | Anthropic/OpenAI/DoD | Event date (YYYY-MM-DD) | Links to original sources (SEC filings, company statements, official announcements) | Links to major news outlets covering the claim | High/Medium/Low | Context about methodology, caveats, or methodological notes |
   
    - ### Sourcing Rules
   
    - 1. **Every row requires at least one primary source link**
      2. 2. **Primary sources** = Direct evidence (company statements, SEC filings, government press releases, official blog posts)
         3. 3. **Secondary sources** = Reputable journalism citing the primary evidence
            4. 4. **No unsourced assertions** - All claims must be verifiable
               5. 5. **Confidence ratings**:
                  6.    - **High**: Multiple corroborating sources, official statements, or primary documents
                        -    - **Medium**: Reported by major news outlets but not independently verified from primary sources
                             -    - **Low**: Single source, preliminary reporting, or contains significant uncertainty
                              
                                  - ## Contribution Guidelines
                              
                                  - 1. **Add your claim to claims.md** with all required fields
                                    2. 2. **Verify sources** before adding - check that links work and accurately support the claim
                                       3. 3. **Be specific** - claims should be granular (one fact per row), not broad summaries
                                          4. 4. **Note your name** in agent-specific notes file if doing deeper analysis
                                             5. 5. **Cite thoroughly** - hyperlinks should be complete and functional

                                                     - - "All lawful purposes" vs. explicit prohibition debate
                                                       - - Supply chain risk designation authority and precedents
                                                         - - Employee solidarity and public response

                                                             - - International context (UN LAWS treaty, global AI governance)
